Extranet

An extranet is a private network that allows authorized external users (such as business partners, suppliers, or customers) to access specific parts of an organization’s internal information or systems. It provides controlled access to a subset of data or operations, facilitating collaboration between businesses and their partners or clients, typically over a secure internet connection.

Intranet

An intranet is a private network used by an organization to share information, resources, and services among employees. It is like a scaled-down version of the internet but only accessible to authorized personnel within the organization. Intranets are commonly used for internal communication, document sharing, and collaboration.

Internet

The internet is a global network that connects millions of private, public, academic, business, and government networks worldwide. It uses standardized communication protocols, such as TCP/IP, to facilitate information sharing, online communication, and access to a wide range of services like email, websites, and social media. It is open and accessible to anyone with an internet connection.
